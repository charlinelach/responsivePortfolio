About Me

When I was in elementary and middle school, I wanted to grow up and become an artist. There was a period of time when I told that to my mother, and she told me I wasn't allowed to pursue such a career. And honestly, I'm glad she told me that.

After considering how hard my mother and father worked at their jobs, I wanted to have a relatively professional, "boring" job. I planned to study technology or business by the time I was a sophomore in high school. First experience of coding: redesigning my Xanga blog UI. The summer before senior year, I went to a week-long summer camp at the University of Minnesota's business school and discovered the Management Information Systems major. It was perfect.

After I had started my career, I discovered that MIS and being an analyst is more business-oriented. I had hoped I studied more coding and have the ability to gain a developer role at some point.
